The Challenge

Epic Reads, HarperCollins' young adult book blog, wasn't short on content. They were publishing more than 10 articles a week, including author spotlights, quizzes, and reading lists. The issue wasn't how much they were creating, it was what they were creating.

Most of the content wasn't built around what people were actually searching for. It worked for existing readers, but it wasn't bringing in new ones, especially people going to Google to figure out what to read next.

When I was brought in to lead SEO, the goals were simple: show up for popular book-related searches, get more people to the product pages, and ultimately, sell more books.

The Solution

Find the Right Keywords

The first step was figuring out what readers were actually searching for. A content gap analysis showed that Epic Reads had almost no posts targeting "best books by genre" searches — queries like "best time travel books," "best heist novels," or "best poetry books for teens." These were a logical fit: people searching that way are actively looking for recommendations, which means they're close to buying. High intent, high relevance, and Epic Reads wasn't showing up for any of it.

A sample of the genre keyword targets identified during research — high search volume, manageable difficulty, and strong purchase intent.

Write for Readers, Optimize for Google

From 50 priority keywords, I built detailed content briefs for the writing team — not just topic overviews, but precise blueprints: recommended outlines, internal linking anchors, metadata guidance, visual direction, and CTA placement. The goal was articles that readers would genuinely enjoy and that Google would reward.

Every piece was optimized across the full range of on-page signals to build keyword relevancy, match search intent, and guide readers naturally toward product pages:

  • Title tags
  • Headers & on-page copy
  • Meta descriptions
  • Internal links
  • Call-to-actions
  • Image alt tags

Example: "14 Time Travel Books That'll Make You Wish You Had a Time Machine" — one of 50+ genre list articles built around high-intent keyword targets.

Get the Technical Fundamentals Right

I also audited the technical side. Each article needed a clean heading hierarchy, an optimized title tag, and metadata that clearly communicated what the page was about. Small things individually — but the signal they send collectively adds up.

Each article was built on clean, semantic HTML — proper heading hierarchy, structured post metadata, and optimized title tags signalling relevance to search engines.

Track, Learn, and Adjust

Publishing was only half the work. Post-launch, I monitored traffic, click-through rates, and conversions on every article. Underperforming pieces got revisited — internal links strengthened from higher-authority pages, meta tags refined, intent re-examined, thin sections expanded, and visual elements added where engagement was dropping off. The strategy stayed alive after publication, not just at the brief stage.

Bonus

Attribution Beyond Google

These articles were also being shared across Epic Reads' social channels. To close the attribution loop, I implemented UTM codes on all social distribution — making it possible to see exactly which posts were driving traffic and sales, down to the individual tweet or Facebook share. That granularity made future content decisions sharper.

The Results

Three months after the strategy launched, epicreads.com had climbed into the top 3 positions on Google for 234 additional keywords — including the most competitive "genre + books" queries where purchase intent is highest.

Organic search traffic grew 97%. That wasn't just a vanity metric — it flowed directly into the bottom line. Year-over-year online book sales increased by 37%. The publishing team was already doing the hard work of creating content; pointing it at the right searches turned their cadence into a revenue engine.

Google Analytics — organic search sessions from October 2019 to March 2020, showing sustained growth following the content strategy launch.
