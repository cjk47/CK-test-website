The Challenge

When Google began rolling out AI Overviews across travel and hospitality queries, Best Western's organic traffic took an immediate hit. Pages that had ranked consistently for years were suddenly displaced by AI-generated answers that didn't reference their content.

The challenge wasn't just recovery — it was adapting to a fundamentally different search landscape where traditional ranking signals weren't enough.

To win in AI search, content needs to be structured, authoritative, and written in a format that AI systems can extract and surface. That required a complete rethinking of how pages were written.

The Strategy

  • AI Overview Content Rewrite

    Rewrote key landing pages to match the structured, answer-forward format that AI Overviews prefer. Clear H2/H3 hierarchies, concise answers to common questions, authoritative language.

  • Schema Markup Implementation

    Implemented Hotel, FAQPage, and LocalBusiness schema across property pages. Structured data gives AI systems clear signals about content context and authority.

  • Topic Cluster & Landing Page Architecture

    I designed a full topic cluster system built around city-level hub pages that link out to targeted subpages — each one capturing a specific traveler need that traditional hotel pages miss entirely.

    Each city hub links down to three tiers of subpages:

    • →Hotels Near [Airport] — dedicated pages for every major airport (e.g., "Hotels Near JFK," "Hotels Near LAX") with distance, shuttle info, and nearby properties.
    • →Hotels Near [Attraction] — pages for key landmarks and venues (e.g., "Hotels Near Madison Square Garden") listing proximity and relevant amenities.
    • →Hotels Near [Destination] — regional subpages (e.g., "Cape Cod Region") covering popular nearby areas.
    • →Themed Pages — intent-specific pages like "Family-Friendly Hotels Near [Attraction]" or "Hotels for Business Travelers in [City]."

    I also built an Events pillar page — a central hub replacing the single overloaded Group & Meeting Reservations page — with dedicated subpages for each event type:

    • →Meetings & Conferences — targeting "conference hotels" and corporate group searches with meeting room details and business-friendly properties.
    • →Celebrations — birthdays, anniversaries, reunions; targeting "hotel party venues" with packages and participating locations.
    • →Group Travel — teams, tour groups, family reunions; group rates, room blocks, and large-group amenities.
    • →Weddings — venues, packages, and guest rooms; targeting "hotel wedding venues" searches.
  • Localized Page Copy

    I rewrote the copy on hotel detail pages to match local search intent — moving beyond generic descriptions to content that references nearby landmarks, popular neighborhoods, and community context specific to each property's location. This gives Google the geographic signals it needs to surface the right hotel for the right local query, and gives travelers the confidence that the property actually understands where it is.

  • FAQ Optimization

    Added optimized FAQ sections to core pages targeting the exact questions travelers ask in search — driving featured snippet and AI Overview capture.

The Results

The content and schema overhaul paid off quickly. AI Overview visibility grew 349% — meaning Best Western pages were now being featured in Google's AI-generated answers for a dramatically larger share of relevant queries.

Organic traffic recovered and surpassed pre-rollout baselines, and the higher-intent visitors coming in through AI search converted at an improved rate.
