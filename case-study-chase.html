The Challenge

The Chase Sapphire Explorer Card page was ranking — but for the wrong things. Search data revealed significant gaps between the queries potential cardholders were using and the content actually on the page.

Comparison queries, benefit-specific searches, and 'is it worth it' questions were all sending traffic to competitors. Internal linking left the card page isolated from supporting content that could boost its authority.

The fix was methodical: surface the gaps, prioritize by search volume and intent, and execute the updates systematically.

The Strategy

  • Search Gap Analysis

    Pulled full search console data and compared ranking queries against the full universe of relevant searches. Identified dozens of high-volume, high-intent queries the page wasn't capturing.

  • On-Page Content Updates

    Rewrote sections of the card page to address comparison queries, benefit breakdowns, and common cardholder questions. Added structured content blocks targeting featured snippets.

  • Metadata Optimization

    Updated title tags and meta descriptions to better match the intent behind high-volume queries — improving click-through rates from existing impressions.

  • Internal Linking Improvements

    Built internal links from high-traffic supporting content to the card page. Improved link equity flow to the primary conversion page.

The Results

Six months of systematic execution produced clear results. Organic clicks to the Explorer Card page grew 69.8%, driven by the page now capturing queries it previously missed entirely.

Impressions grew 21.3% as the page began ranking for a broader set of relevant queries — validating the content gap analysis and the on-page updates that followed.
