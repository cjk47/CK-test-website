The Challenge

XVIVO Biomedical Animation creates world-class scientific visualizations for pharmaceutical and biotech companies. The work is stunning. The problem? The right buyers couldn't find them.

Their site had generic metadata, thin page descriptions, and content structured for a broad audience rather than the high-intent decision-makers they actually serve. Crawl coverage was inconsistent and internal linking left key service pages buried.

In a niche B2B market where a single client relationship is worth hundreds of thousands of dollars, invisibility in search was costing them real revenue.

The Strategy

  • Technical SEO Audit

    Crawled the entire site to surface indexing issues, redirect chains, and pages leaking PageRank. Fixed 30+ technical issues blocking proper crawl coverage.

  • Metadata Overhaul

    Rewrote title tags and meta descriptions across 40+ pages to match buyer intent — pharmaceutical companies searching for specific animation types and deliverables.

  • Topic Cluster Strategy

    I built a topic cluster architecture around medical animation — creating dedicated service pages for every major animation type: mechanism of action, neurology, oncology, clinical trial recruitment, patient education, and more. Each page targets its own set of long-tail queries while reinforcing XVIVO's topical authority across the entire medical animation category. The result: Google treats XVIVO as the definitive source on the subject, lifting rankings across the board.

  • Portfolio Crawlability

    XVIVO's portfolio filtered views relied on URL parameters, making them invisible to search engines. I identified the opportunity to convert each filter into a standalone, indexable page — allowing XVIVO to directly target high-intent long-tail searches like "neurology medical animation" or "clinical trial recruitment animation."

    • →Convert filtered views into unique, indexable URLs with clean descriptive paths instead of parameter-based URLs.
    • →Write unique introductory copy for each portfolio category explaining the animation type and typical use cases — no reused or generic copy across pages.
    • →Align page titles, headings, body copy, and metadata to the target query for each category.
    • →Link to category pages from relevant service and blog pages using descriptive anchor text to reinforce topical relevance.
  • Search Intent Alignment

    Rankings mean nothing if the page doesn't match what the visitor actually wants. I audited each key page against its dominant search intent and recommended content changes accordingly. On the homepage, for example, users searching for a medical animation company want to see the work — not read about it. Surfacing actual video examples above the fold reduced bounce signals and gave Google stronger behavioral confirmation that the page deserved to rank.

  • Internal Linking Architecture

    Restructured internal links to flow authority toward core service pages and reduce click depth to key conversion points.

The Results

Within three months of implementation, XVIVO's organic performance showed clear, measurable improvement across every tracked metric.

The content and technical fixes compounded quickly — more pages indexed correctly meant more impressions, better metadata meant better click-through rates, and better landing pages meant more inquiries.
